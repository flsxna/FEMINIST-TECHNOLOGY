Women in the tech industry

Until 2021, women are still underrepresented in the technology industry particularly
in leadership roles. According to data from the National Center for Women &
Information Technology, women hold only 25% of computing roles in the US, and only
5% of tech startups are owned by women. Additionally, women in tech often
experience discrimination and bias in the workplace, including lower salaries and
limited career advancement opportunities.
The "Elephant in the Valley" survey was conducted in 2016 and gathered responses
from over 200 women who had worked in Silicon Valley for at least 10 years. And it
found that 60% of women in tech had experienced unwanted sexual advances, 87%
had witnessed sexist behavior in the workplace, and 59% have felt they have not had
the same opportunities as their male counterparts.
"The Leaky Tech Pipeline" report by the Kapor Center examines that women and
people of color are underrepresented in tech and highlights the experiences of those
who have faced discrimination and bias in the industry. They provide the data that less
than 1 in 5 techcompanies are founded by women, only 18% of women earn CS
degrees and only 20% of them are Black/Latinx.
